# Motivation Letter for Recent Topics in Business Informatics

My chosen topic of interest is Topic III - DGS: "The use of LLMs in the hiring process".

This topic is of prime interest to me because I have been applying to jobs, both with the help of LLMs (updating my CV, having them write cover letter) and without (completely manually writing and updating my profile). I have so far had very little success and seriously doubt the use of this technology by both applicants and recruiters. Having worked for more than 3 years, as a software engineer I was hired before LLMs became ubiquitous in the hiring process, and seeing how difficult it has currently become to break into the German job market, I wish to answer the following research question:

> <u>[Research Question]</u>
> "Are LLMs muddying up the hiring process and making it harder for applicants to get hired?"

As companies increasingly incorporate LLMs in their Applicant Tracking Systems of their hiring pipelines<sup>1</sup> it becomes obvious to ask what effects this would have and whether it is a good idea to have automated systems judge the suitability of people and their potential livelihoods. Does usage of LLMs by both sides (applicant and recruiter) lead to "Garbage in garbage out", with neither side seeing the benefits? I intend to perform structured literature review on this issue. And aim to figure out if something as important as a person's livelihood should depend upon the individual's skills or whether they have access to the state-of-the-art LLM assisted tools<sup>2</sup>.
